# Visual Comparison Matrices - User Guide

## 📊 Overview

The Visual Comparison tab provides 3 interactive scatter plot matrices to help you compare universities across multiple dimensions simultaneously.

---

## 1️⃣ Cost vs Quality Matrix

### What It Shows
- **X-axis:** Total Program Cost (tuition + living × duration)
- **Y-axis:** University Tier (1 = Best, 3 = Lower)
- **Bubble Size:** Number of available scholarships
- **Color:** Region

### How to Use
1. **Look for bottom-left quadrant:** Low cost + High quality (Tier 1) = Best value
2. **Check bubble size:** Larger bubbles = more scholarship options
3. **Compare regions:** See which regions offer better value

### Key Insights
- **Best Value:** ETH Zurich (Tier 1, ~$47k total), Zhejiang Hangzhou (Tier 1, ~$45k total)
- **Premium but Funded:** MIT, Stanford, Oxford (Tier 1, $160-180k but many scholarships)
- **Mid-Range:** TUM Munich, NUS (Tier 2, $54-83k)

### Decision Guide
- **If self-funding:** Choose bottom-left (low cost, high quality)
- **If scholarship-dependent:** Choose large bubbles (many options)
- **If prestige matters:** Choose Tier 1 regardless of cost

## 2️⃣ Research vs Industry Matrix

### What It Shows
- **X-axis:** Research Intensity (Low → Very High)
- **Y-axis:** Industry Connections (Limited → Excellent)
- **Bubble Size:** Post-graduation salary
- **Color:** Region

### How to Use
1. **Identify quadrants:**
   - **Top-Right (Hybrid):** Strong in both research and industry
   - **Bottom-Right (Research):** PhD pipeline, academic careers
   - **Top-Left (Industry):** Job-ready, practical skills
   - **Bottom-Left:** Avoid (weak in both)

2. **Match to your goals:**
   - Want PhD? → Look right (high research)
   - Want job immediately? → Look up (excellent industry)
   - Unsure? → Choose top-right (hybrid)

3. **Check salary:** Larger bubbles = higher post-grad salary

### Key Insights

**Hybrid (Top-Right) - Best of Both:**
- MIT, Stanford, CMU (USA)
- Zhejiang (China)
- NUS (Singapore)
- **Best for:** Flexible career paths

**Research-Focused (Bottom-Right) - PhD Pipeline:**
- Oxford, Cambridge (UK)
- Tsinghua, Peking, SJTU (China)
- **Best for:** Academic careers, research labs

**Industry-Focused (Top-Left) - Job-Ready:**
- Imperial (UK)
- TUM Munich (Germany)
- SUSTech (China)
- **Best for:** Immediate employment

### Decision Guide
- **Path A (LPDP - Return to Indonesia):** Choose research-focused or hybrid (prestige matters)
- **Path B (International Career):** Choose industry-focused or hybrid (skills matter)
- **PhD aspirations:** Choose research-focused or hybrid (publications matter)

## 3️⃣ Salary vs Visa Difficulty Matrix

### What It Shows
- **X-axis:** Post-Graduation Salary (USD)
- **Y-axis:** Visa Difficulty (Easy → Very Hard)
- **Bubble Size:** Total program cost
- **Color:** Region

### How to Use
1. **Look for top-left quadrant:** High salary + Easy visa = Ideal!
2. **Assess risk tolerance:**
   - Risk-averse? → Choose "Easy" or "Medium" visa
   - Risk-tolerant? → Consider "Hard" or "Very Hard" if salary justifies
3. **Consider long-term:** Easy visa = faster path to permanent residence

### Key Insights

**Best Balance (Top-Left):**
- TUM Munich (Germany): €72k salary, Easy visa
- NUS (Singapore): $70k salary, Easy visa
- **Why good:** Reasonable salary + clear immigration path

**High Risk/High Reward (Bottom-Right):**
- MIT, Stanford, CMU (USA): $140-250k salary, Very Hard visa (H-1B lottery)
- **Why risky:** Amazing salary but uncertain visa outcome

**Moderate Options (Middle):**
- Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial (UK): £60-120k salary, Medium visa
- ETH Zurich (Switzerland): CHF 124k salary, Hard visa (but worth it)
- **Why moderate:** Good salary, possible but requires effort

**China (Bottom-Middle):**
- Tsinghua, Zhejiang (China): $62-75k salary, Hard visa
- **Why challenging:** Good salary but nearly impossible to get permanent residence

### Decision Guide
- **Want stability:** Choose Easy visa countries (Germany, Singapore)
- **Want highest salary:** Accept Very Hard visa (USA) but have backup plan
- **Want balance:** Choose Medium visa countries (UK, Netherlands)
- **Path A (LPDP):** Visa difficulty less relevant (must return anyway)

## 📋 Quick Comparison Table

The table at the bottom shows all universities sorted by ROI (Return on Investment):
- **ROI = Salary - Total Cost**
- Higher ROI = Better financial outcome

### How to Use
1. **Sort by ROI:** See which universities give best financial returns
2. **Filter by region:** Focus on regions you're interested in
3. **Check all dimensions:** Cost, Salary, Research, Industry, Visa

### Key Insights
- **Highest ROI:** Usually USA universities (high salary despite high cost)
- **Best Value:** ETH Zurich, Zhejiang (low cost + good salary)
- **Lowest ROI:** Expensive programs with moderate salaries

## 💡 How to Use All 3 Matrices Together

### Step 1: Start with Cost vs Quality
- Identify universities in your budget
- Check scholarship availability (bubble size)
- Shortlist 5-10 universities

### Step 2: Check Research vs Industry
- Match universities to your career goals
- PhD? → Choose right side (high research)
- Job? → Choose top side (excellent industry)
- Unsure? → Choose top-right (hybrid)
- Narrow to 3-5 universities

### Step 3: Assess Salary vs Visa
- Check career feasibility
- Consider visa difficulty vs salary trade-off
- Think long-term (permanent residence)
- Make final decision

## 🎯 Example Decision Flows

### Example 1: Budget-Conscious, Want PhD
1. **Cost vs Quality:** Look for Tier 1 universities under $100k
   - ETH Zurich, Zhejiang, Tsinghua
2. **Research vs Industry:** Choose high research intensity
   - All three are high research
3. **Salary vs Visa:** Check visa difficulty
   - ETH: Hard visa but high salary
   - Zhejiang: Hard visa, moderate salary
   - **Decision:** ETH Zurich (best value + research)

### Example 2: Want High Salary, Risk-Tolerant
1. **Cost vs Quality:** Focus on Tier 1, cost less important
   - MIT, Stanford, CMU
2. **Research vs Industry:** Choose hybrid (flexibility)
   - All three are hybrid
3. **Salary vs Visa:** Accept Very Hard visa for $200k+ salary
   - **Decision:** Stanford (highest salary + Silicon Valley)

### Example 3: Want Job Immediately, Stability
1. **Cost vs Quality:** Moderate budget, Tier 2 acceptable
   - TUM Munich, Imperial, NUS
2. **Research vs Industry:** Choose excellent industry connections
   - All three have excellent industry
3. **Salary vs Visa:** Prioritize Easy visa
   - TUM: Easy visa, €72k
   - NUS: Easy visa, $70k
   - Imperial: Medium visa, £75k
   - **Decision:** TUM Munich (easy visa + good salary)

### Example 4: LPDP Scholarship (Path A)
1. **Cost vs Quality:** Cost irrelevant (fully funded), focus on Tier 1
   - MIT, Stanford, Oxford, Cambridge, Tsinghua
2. **Research vs Industry:** Choose research prestige (Indonesian employers value)
   - Oxford, Cambridge (very high research)
   - MIT, Stanford (high research + industry)
3. **Salary vs Visa:** Visa irrelevant (must return anyway)
   - **Decision:** MIT or Oxford (prestige + LPDP network)

## 🔍 Interactive Features

### Hover for Details
- Hover over any bubble to see:
  - University name
  - Exact values (cost, salary, etc.)
  - Additional attributes

### Zoom and Pan
- Click and drag to zoom into specific areas
- Double-click to reset view
- Use toolbar to pan, zoom, or reset

### Filter by Region
- Click on region in legend to hide/show
- Double-click region to show only that region
- Useful for comparing within regions

### Download Charts
- Click camera icon to save chart as PNG
- Share with advisors, family, or friends

## ⚠️ Important Considerations

### Limitations
1. **Salaries are estimates:** Actual salaries vary by specialization, company, experience
2. **Costs are approximate:** Living costs vary by lifestyle, location within city
3. **Visa difficulty is general:** Individual circumstances vary (nationality, profile, timing)
4. **Scholarship availability changes:** Check current year availability

### Not Shown in Matrices
- Program curriculum details
- Faculty expertise
- Alumni network strength
- Campus culture and fit
- Specific research areas
- Industry partnerships details

### Use Matrices As
- ✅ **Starting point** for shortlisting
- ✅ **Visual comparison** tool
- ✅ **Trade-off analysis** aid

### Don't Use Matrices As
- ❌ **Only decision factor**
- ❌ **Replacement for research**
- ❌ **Guarantee of outcomes**
