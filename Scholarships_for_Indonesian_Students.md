## Indonesian Government Scholarships

### 1. LPDP (Lembaga Pengelola Dana Pendidikan) Scholarship ⭐ HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

**Overview:**
The Indonesian government's flagship scholarship program for studying abroad or domestically. Expanded intake for 2026 with 5,750+ scholarships available.

**Coverage:**
- Full tuition fees
- Monthly living allowance
- Return airfare/transport
- Book allowance
- Visa and health insurance
- Registration fees

**Eligible Destinations:**
- United States (MIT, Stanford, etc.)
- United Kingdom (Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial)
- Europe (ETH Zurich, TUM, Netherlands universities)
- Australia (Monash, etc.)
- Singapore (NUS)
- Top 200 preferred partner institutions globally

**Key Requirements:**
- Indonesian citizenship
- Bachelor's degree
- Strong academic record
- Commitment to return to Indonesia after studies
- IELTS/TOEFL scores for international programs

**Application Process:**
- Online application during designated periods
- Upload required documents
- Pay application fee online
- Rigorous selection process

**Special Programs:**

#### LPDP-Netherlands Joint Scholarship
- Partnership with Dutch universities
- Double the number of scholarships for Indonesian applicants
- Focus on Cultural Heritage programs
- University of Twente is a preferred partner (top 200 globally)

#### LPDP-Australia Awards Scholarships
- Joint program with Australian government
- Target areas for 2026:
  - **Blue economy**
  - **Green/renewable energy**
  - **Digital/information technology** ← Relevant for AI
- Open to Indonesians from diverse backgrounds
- Priority for equity groups (women, people with disabilities, targeted provinces)

**Application Timeline:**
- Check [LPDP official website](https://lpdp.kemenkeu.go.id) for specific deadlines
- Typically opens early in the year

**Official Resources:**
- Website: lpdp.kemenkeu.go.id
- DAAD Indonesia portal for European options

## International Scholarships for Indonesian Citizens

### 2. Chevening Scholarship (UK) ⭐ HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

**Overview:**
UK government's prestigious scholarship program for future leaders. Fully funded for one-year Master's programs.

**Coverage:**
- Full university tuition fees
- Monthly living allowance
- Economy class return airfare to UK
- Additional grants for essential expenditure
- Visa costs
- Travel grants to Chevening events

**Eligible Programs:**
- Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial, UCL, Edinburgh
- Any UK university offering AI/ML Master's programs
- One-year intensive programs

**Requirements:**
- Indonesian citizenship (Chevening-eligible country)
- Undergraduate degree qualifying for UK postgraduate study
- **Minimum 2 years (2,800 hours) work experience**
- Commitment to return to Indonesia for at least 2 years after scholarship
- Strong leadership potential
- No British citizenship or dual British citizenship

**Application Strategy:**
- Need compelling story and leadership evidence
- Strategic course selection (can apply to 3 programs)
- Strong references
- Clear career plan showing impact in Indonesia

**Application Status:**
- **Applications for 2026-2027 are NOW OPEN**
- Apply at: [chevening.org/scholarship/indonesia](https://www.chevening.org/scholarship/indonesia)

**Timeline:**
- Applications typically close in November
- Interviews in February-April
- Results announced in June

### 3. Australia Awards Scholarships ⭐ HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

**Overview:**
Australian government's flagship scholarship for developing countries. Fully funded for Master's and PhD programs.

**Coverage:**
- Full tuition fees
- Return economy airfare
- Establishment allowance (initial costs)
- Fortnightly Contribution to Living Expenses (CLE)
- Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)
- Introductory Academic Program (IAP)
- Pre-course English (PCE) fees if needed

**Eligible Programs:**
- Any Australian university
- Master's and Doctoral degrees
- AI, Computer Science, Data Science programs

**Special Features:**
- Some programs offer dual degrees (e.g., 1 year at Universitas Airlangga + 1 year at University of Western Australia)

**Requirements:**
- Indonesian citizenship
- Bachelor's degree
- Strong academic record
- Commitment to return to Indonesia
- Priority for equity groups (women from disadvantaged backgrounds, people with disabilities, targeted provinces)

**Application Status:**
- **Applications NOW OPEN for 2026 intake**
- Opened February 1, 2026

**Official Website:**
- [australiaawardsindonesia.org](https://www.australiaawardsindonesia.org/)
- Australian Embassy Jakarta

### 4. DAAD Scholarships (Germany) ⭐ EXCELLENT VALUE

**Overview:**
German Academic Exchange Service offers 10,000+ scholarships annually. Excellent for studying at TUM, RWTH Aachen, TU Berlin, etc.

**Programs:**

#### DAAD EPOS (Development-Related Postgraduate Courses)
- For professionals from developing countries
- Focus on sustainable development
- Fully funded

#### DAAD Study Scholarships for STEM Disciplines
- Specifically for STEM fields including AI
- Innovation-driving fields
- Support qualification and employability

**Coverage:**
- Monthly allowance: €992 (Master's) or €1,400 (PhD)
- Annual study allowance: €460
- Tuition fees (where applicable)
- Health insurance
- Travel allowance
- Rent subsidies
- Family allowances (if applicable)
- Special support for disabilities/chronic illnesses

**Eligible Universities:**
- Technical University of Munich (TUM)
- RWTH Aachen University
- TU Berlin
- University of Freiburg
- All German universities offering relevant programs

**Requirements:**
- Bachelor's degree
- Strong academic record
- Relevant work experience (varies by program)
- German or English language proficiency (depending on program)

**Application:**
- Can apply to maximum 3 postgraduate courses
- List in order of preference
- Keep order consistent throughout application

**Resources:**
- [daad-indonesia.org](https://www.daad-indonesia.org/en/)
- Scholarship database available on website

**Eligible Fields:**
- STEM fields including AI, Computer Science, Data Science

- Living stipend

- Book allowance

**Requirements:**
- Indonesian citizenship
- Bachelor's degree
- Strong academic record

- Typically 7 years or less professional experience
- Commitment to return to Indonesia

**Application:**
- Through AMINEF (American Indonesian Exchange Foundation)
- Competitive selection process

**Note:** Check specific deadlines and requirements at AMINEF website

- Build work experience (for Chevening)
- Strengthen academic profile

**12 months before:**
- Take English proficiency tests
- Request recommendation letters

**9-6 months before:**
- Apply for LPDP (check specific deadlines)
- Apply for Chevening (typically August-November)
- Apply for Australia Awards (typically February-April)
- Apply for DAAD (varies by program)
- Apply for Fulbright (check AMINEF deadlines)

**6-3 months before:**

- Prepare for interviews

- Secure conditional offers

**Tier 1 - Apply First (Highest Success Rate for Indonesians):**
1. **LPDP** - Indonesian government priority, expanded intake

**Tier 3 - Supplementary Options:**
7. **University-specific scholarships** - Apply directly through universities

**For LPDP:**
- Strong commitment to Indonesia's development
- Clear plan for post-study contribution
- Align with national priorities (digital technology, AI for development)
- Network with LPDP alumni

**For Chevening:**
- Demonstrate leadership experience
- Show clear career progression
- Articulate how UK study benefits Indonesia
- Strong networking and influence potential

**For Australia Awards:**
- Emphasize development impact
- If from equity groups, highlight this
- Focus on blue economy, green energy, or digital tech for 2026
- Show commitment to Indonesia's development goals

**For DAAD:**
- Strong academic record essential
- Research German universities thoroughly
- Consider learning basic German (helpful but not always required)
- Align with sustainable development goals

### Work Experience Consideration

**Scholarships Requiring Work Experience:**
- Chevening: 2 years minimum (2,800 hours)

- LPDP: Preferred but not always required

**Fresh Graduates Can Apply:**

## Important Notes

1. **Multiple Applications:** You can apply to multiple scholarships simultaneously. Many students apply to 3-5 programs.

2. **Conditional Offers:** Some scholarships require university admission first, others handle it together.

3. **Return Commitment:** Most scholarships require you to return to Indonesia for 2+ years. Plan accordingly.

4. **English Proficiency:** Start IELTS/TOEFL preparation early. Most programs require:
   - IELTS: 7.0-7.5
   - TOEFL iBT: 100+

5. **Work Experience:** If you're a fresh graduate, focus on LPDP, Fulbright, DAAD, and Australia Awards. Build experience for Chevening.

6. **Application Quality:** Better to apply to fewer scholarships with high-quality applications than many with rushed applications.

7. **Networking:** Connect with scholarship alumni through LinkedIn, Indonesian student associations abroad.
